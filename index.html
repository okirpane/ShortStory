WANDA’s DOG

Wanda stared in disbelief at the creature before her.

A panting, slobbering humanoid dog creature sitting wrapped in blankets with wide eyes hungry for attention and love. It’s head was that of a dog, just three times larger. It’s body resembled Wanda’s own, except for the hideous fur that grew in wet clumps randomly over her body.

“It’s gross.” Wanda remarked harshly.

The thing whimpered at her cold tone. Maybe, it was the thunder and rain outside. Wanda couldn’t tell.

“Don’t be rude, Wanda!” her mom admonished in a whisper as she brought a bowl of soup from the kitchen and placed it gently in front of the creature.

“Drink this. You’ll feel better.”

Her mom sat next to it and wrapped her hands around it in a comforting embrace. Wanda gaped at the affection her mother was showering on the creature. No, she was more surprised by the total lack of disgust her parents showed. Did they not see what she was seeing?

Did they not see its drooling, ravenous maw? Or it’s dirty matted fur?

Did they not see the beast invading their warm, safe living room and their perfectly happy family?

Wanda sat on the simple warm rug right by the fireplace, still not completely coming to terms with the reality she was in. This repulsive alien was being coddled by both her parents, who didn’t seem to recognise the utter bizarreness and inhumanity of it. They just accepted this creature, that had showed up at their doorstep. Feeding it food, keeping it warm and comforting it as best as they could.

Her parents ignored her confounded looks and eventually took the creature upstairs leaving Wanda alone, crackling with anger and drifting to sleep by the fireplace.
        The next morning the creature had a name.

“Her name is Regina.” Her dad said at the breakfast table, “ We are… taking her in.”

“Be nice to her, okay?”

Wanda did not reply. instead, she wolfed down her breakfast and hurried to school. She couldn’t wait to tell her friends about the bizarre creature in her house.

When she regaled her absurd story, she was met with awe and intrigue. Everyone gathered around during the lunch break to hear more. She was the center of attention. Everytime she told the story to a new curious classmate, it changed, and Regina became more and more grotesque.

Wanda went back home with a euphoric smile on her face. She reluctantly tore herself away from the loving audience that had followed her from lunch break, to art class, to drama rehearsal, to the school bus, and skipped back home up to her front door. Her mom opened the door, greeted her and quickly disappeared back into the house.

“How was school today?” Wanda’s mom asked.

“It was great!” Wanda said with a smile on her face.

She took off her shoes and socks leaving them both strewn near the door which she left clumsily half open. Wanda bounced into the kitchen of their house, which was small but neatly stocked with snacks, produce and a certain comfortable warmth. She grabbed a cookie and carried it out into the living room clenched between her teeth. She dropped it when she gaped at the scene before her.

“What’s going on?”

Her dad was sitting with the thing at their rickety old piano, teaching it how to play a simple tune. Her mom was stirring her tea and humming along whenever it played something resembling a melody. It looked comfortable in the house, playing along and lapping up the attention it was getting.

“What’s wrong?” Her mom asked jovially, distracted by the creatures stumbling performance.

All of Wanda’s joy was flushed out and replaced with disgust. She didn’t want to see this inhuman creature happy.

“That’s my Piano!” Wanda cried.

“I know! Isn’t she playing so well?” Her dad responded with laughter and an encouraging pat on Regina’s back.

“She can’t use it. It’s mine. I need it!” Wanda tried to control her tone, but she could feel rage creeping into her voice.

“Honey, sit down. You must’ve had a stressful day.”

“My day was fine. I want my piano back, I need it for… practice.”

“You can practice later, right?” Her dad said chuckling at another one of Regina’s fumbles.

“No! I need it right now!” Wanda shouted, “What does it need the piano for anyway?” Her parents’ grins faded and her father rose to walk up to her.

“What did you say?” He said.

“I said what does a… thing like that need the piano for?”

Her dad glowered down at her, but she stood strong. Just because her parents were clearly delusional didn’t mean she had to stoop to their level. She wasn’t stupid.

“Apologize.” He said curtly, “Apologize right now.”

“No.”

“Say you’re sorry, and that you’ll never say something like that again.”

“No.”

“Say sorry.” Her dad shouted, “ NOW!”

“I WON’T.” Wanda screamed, “YOU BOTH ARE CLEARLY… STUPID!”

Wanda stormed off. As she marched upstairs to her bedroom she could hear whimpers from the creature, distracting her parents. They both forgot about her, turning their attention towards the plight of their new child. Leaving Wanda, once again, alone, sobbing and screaming with anger.

A few mornings later the creature was coming to school with her. Wearing her old shirt and jeans. Slurping milk out of a kiddie bowl at the table, almost oblivious to the strife she was causing.

“Treat her well.” Her dad said, “Introduce her to your friends.”

“Don't let anyone bully her.” Her mom added

“Don't bully her either.”

Wanda didn't reply. She just got up, put her bowl in the sink and grabbed her bag. As Wanda stormed out the door her mom called after her, admonishing her rudeness but faltered for some reason.

“I can’t remember her…” Her mom tried to say something, but Wanda was already out the door. There was time till the bus showed, so she decided to walk to school to get away from home as quickly as possible. When she reached, classes were about to start, and her peers had already been introduced to Regina.

“That’s her?” Wanda’s best friend Linda asked.

“That’s it.” Wanda corrected.

“But…” Linda craned her neck to look closer at the thing sitting in the front row. “She looks normal. I thought you said she was a monster!”

“What? Of course she’s a monster! You think a human-dog thing is normal?”

Linda stared at Linda now. As if she was the weird one.

“You’re mad.”

Wanda wasn’t mad, everyone else was. They all loved the thing and over the next months and semesters, their love increased. Wanda was outshone and outmatched in everything.

Everytime Regina topped another class test she had to watch her class applaud it. She had to watch Regina topple her as the queen of drama club, serenading everyone with her inhumanely perfect voice. She had to watch her parents slowly forget her name. She felt the pride drain out of their eyes when she walked into the room.

Everything was about Regina and there was nothing Wanda could do about it. She could feel Regina laughing at her behind her back. It had to be behind her back because in front of her, Regina only had the guts to be the most artificially sweet thing in the world. Wanda knew what it was, but no one else did. That’s why everyone thought she was completely unhinged from reality. Her parents sent her to a doctor to see if anything was wrong,  But Wanda refused to talk to anyone anymore much less some quack doctor. She decided to block everyone out.

She looked at her feet when she spoke, and refused to register anyone’s face when she walked through the corridors at school. Everyone was sick of her raving about dogs and monsters, and she was sick of everyone calling her mad. Wanda had become an outcast, both by choice and by her peers.

That’s why she was surprised to see one of her friends Linda knocking at her door.

“Hey.” She said to Linda as she opened the door for her.

“Hey.” Linda said awkwardly. She hadn’t really talked with Wanda in months.

“What are you doing here?”

“I’m...I’m actually here to hangout with...Regina? Is she home?”

Wanda was surprised. “Yeah, it-she’s in her room.”

        “You still don’t like her?” Linda asks with a smile. Wanda doesn’t reply. She just let’s Linda in and shuts the door. Wanda politely takes Linda’s coat and hangs it.

        “You know me.”

        “She’s just a girl you know. She’s nice. Just give her a chance-”

        “No. I’m good.”

        “You can hangout with us if you want?”

        “I have work to do. You guys have fun.”

Linda sighed. “You’re m-”

        “Yes, I’m Mad. I know.” Wanda gives her a wry smile and walks up to her room.

She missed Linda. She missed her badly. Should she go back and give Linda a hug? Apologize for ending their friendship and beg of her to take her back, to laugh and play with her again? Take a step towards rebuilding their friendship and lifting herself from the misery of isolation? Wanda got hopeful. But then, she realised. That would mean interacting with Regina again. That would mean treating that monster like a human. She would have to look at the slobbering mutt who had uprooted her life, point at it, and call it a friend.

So, she went to it’s room and knocked on the door. Regina swung it open and looked surprised.

“You’re… why’re you here?”

“I don’t… Can I… I’m…” Wanda stuttered, “I wanted to apologize to Linda. And, maybe…”

“Mom and dad aren’t at home. You don’t need to be nice.”

“No, I’m not trying to be nice. I… Just want to hangout.”

She walked into the room, sat down and gradually joined in with their laughter and fun. Wanda apologized for her cruel words. She hugged Linda, and smiled at Regina. They reciprocated. She apologized profusely for her cruel actions and words. They accepted it and roped her into the evening they had planned.

Initially she felt awkward. Her social skills had been atrophied by her social exile. She stuttered a lot, and sometimes let out utter nonsense. But her stiff conversation was smoothed over by the trashy pop the two other girls were playing in the background. Their words didn’t feel plastic or insulting. Her words became kinder and lively.

After eating copious amounts of ice cream they looked out the window and noticed the sky teetering towards night. Linda said she had to leave, but stayed a bit longer till she could hear her dad honking impatiently from the driveway.

“This was surreal…” Wanda remarked as Linda admonished her dad and told him to wait for sometime.

“Yeah, but it was nice. I don’t think I can remember the last time we talked this much.” Linda said putting her coat on.

“I don’t think I’ve ever talked this much with you.” Regina added. She looked like she wanted to give Wanda a small hug but she stopped herself. That would take time.

“Don’t be a stranger!” Linda shouted hurriedly running towards the driveway waving back at them.

Regina sighed and smiled at Wanda. Wanda smiled back, if only slightly. Now that Linda was gone, the atmosphere was a little wooden but, not malicious. So, they both decided to be quiet. Just sit in each other’s company for a bit. Wanda made them both tea, and propped herself up on the plushy red sofa with her feet hanging off the side.

“Thank you.” Wanda said to Regina nursing the warm cup in her hand

“For what?” Regina asked.

“For today.”

“No problem.” Regina said, as it lounged down on the soft carpet, “Never knew you could be nice once in a while.”

“No problem. ” She paused and stretched her arms, “I really need something to eat.” Regina nodded contently.

Wanda got up to go to the kitchen. She loved today and she was grateful. She felt happy, and included for once in a very long time. More importantly it helped her come to a decision she was debating internally for a long time. Regina was a sweetheart, and had almost immediately accepted her. They had an easy rhythm between them. Despite the hate, when they spoke as friends there was no tension. Wanda was grateful that she took the chance to meet it with an open mind with one of her bestfriends. If this didn’t quell her hate for the creature nothing would.

So she walked out of the kitchen with a steel heart and mind. It looked up at her and smiled before morphing into an expression of confusion. Wanda took the sharpest knife she could find in the kitchen and gripped it tightly in her hands.

“You still disgust me.”

Wanda bounded forward and pinned the creature down, raising the knife over her head. Her mind was calm and without doubt. She knew that despite the creatures clever act. Despite it’s inerrant act as a perfect daughter and student. Despite it’s humanity, this thing would only ever be a creature. Something that had taken her normal life from her and had replaced it with misery. This mutt was a threat, she had no doubt.

She had no doubt when she brought down the knife on the thrashing alien under her. Her grip was firm, and her arm was strong. Her mind was already feeling lighter, free from the torment this creature had inflicted upon her.

She had no doubt until she looked down at the creature bathed in blood to see, not a monster but a helpless, frail girl lying on the floor. She was mistaken. The shock of her assault had shaken her out of the jealous illusion she had constructed for herself.

But, the deed was done, the knife was buried and the blood was on her hands. And, no matter how hard she tried, it would not come off.
